01
AI Visibility · 2024–25

How I made a personal care brand the answer, not just a result

The problem

The brand had decent SEO rankings but was completely invisible in AI-generated answers, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews weren't citing them at all. As search behaviour shifted toward LLMs, their organic traffic was quietly bleeding out.

What I did

Audited their entire content architecture through the lens of LLM retrieval, not traditional search. Built entity authority from scratch: structured their brand as an unambiguous knowledge graph node, rewrote key pages to be RAG-retrievable, and designed a topical cluster map that made them the definitive source in their category. Unified their SEO and GEO strategies into one compounding system.

The result

Top LLM citations across major AI answer engines in their product category. Brand began appearing in ChatGPT and Perplexity responses without paid promotion, pure authority.

Fintech · Bajaj Finance · 2021–24

Taking EMI card acquisition from near-zero to a real growth channel

The problem

EMI Card and Health EMI Card acquisition was underperforming badly, a high-potential product with a broken discovery and onboarding journey. The funnel was leaking at every stage and no single team owned the end-to-end problem.

What I did

Took ownership of the full product roadmap, from acquisition triggers to activation. Mapped every drop-off point in the user journey, re-architected the superapp's search and navigation so the product was actually discoverable, and coordinated delivery across a dozen cross-functional teams simultaneously. Wrote the specs, ran the standups, held the accountability.

The result

Acquisition scaled from near-zero to a channel that moved the needle at India's largest NBFC. Shipped a complex regulatory feature in days that the org had quoted months for. Recognised in BFL 30u30, one of the youngest in the group.

03
GenAI · Accenture · Apr–May 2025

Building a GenAI modernisation framework that could actually survive a regulator

The problem

A major bank wanted to modernise core banking with GenAI but had no framework for doing it safely. Every vendor pitch looked good in a deck, none of them addressed regulatory risk, legacy system entanglement, or what happens when a model makes a wrong call on a credit decision.

What I did

Ran structured stakeholder workshops to surface the real constraints, not just the stated ones. Built a 7R modernisation playbook (Rehost, Replatform, Refactor, Rearchitect, Replace, Retire, Retain) grounded in global benchmarks. Embedded human-in-the-loop governance at every decision point where regulatory exposure was real. Designed the framework to be auditable, not just presentable.

The result

A GenAI modernisation roadmap that stakeholders could actually commit to, not because it was optimistic, but because it was honest about risk. Framework built to outlast the project team that built it.
